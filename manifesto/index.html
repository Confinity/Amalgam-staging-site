Manifesto

What we believe about software, agents, and the teams that ship them.

01

Software is about to change more in five years than it did in the last twenty.

Agents are already writing code, answering customers, making decisions. The teams that win next won't be the ones with the biggest models. They'll be the ones with the clearest systems.

02

Speed without systems is just noise that's expensive to clean up.

We've seen the pattern. A great demo, a frantic launch, six months of rework. We'd rather ship in week three with a plan you can still run in month six.

03

Security isn't something you add at the end. It's how you decide what to build.

If your compliance team can't defend it, it shouldn't be in production. Not eventually. Not ever.

04

Agents don't remove the need for humans. They change what humans do.

Every system we build assumes a human is in the loop at the right moment. Approving, escalating, overriding. Not watching dashboards. Making calls.

05

The best software feels like the team that built it.

If your team is careful, the product is careful. If your team is clear, the product is clear. We build with your people because that's who the thing has to survive.

06

You should keep what we built when we leave.

Architecture maps. Eval harnesses. Release checklists. Dashboards your team can read at 2am. We're not trying to stay. We're trying to be worth recommending.

07

Boring words. Interesting outcomes.

We don't need to dress the work up. A shipped feature beats a better deck every time. If we're explaining ourselves in slides, we've already lost the room.

08

There's no such thing as a small promise.

If we say we'll email you on Tuesday, we email you on Tuesday. If we say a system can handle it, the system can handle it. We'd rather say less and be right.

Our commitment

If the first two weeks of a Founder Review don't give you a plan you can actually run, or the honest answer that you don't need us, we refund it.

We'd rather lose a fee than sell you a plan you can't use.

Tell us the blocker. We'll tell you the move.
