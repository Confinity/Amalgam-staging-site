Why the old setup stopped fitting

M&T Bank's business banking platform operated on Voyager, a legacy system. When the vendor announced discontinuation of support, M&T faced a mission-critical decision: modernize or risk operational vulnerabilities.

The work had to preserve continuity while giving the team a cleaner path forward.

M&T Bank is one of the largest commercial banks in the United States, offering financial solutions across retail, commercial, and institutional markets. The bank needed to modernize its digital infrastructure to better serve evolving customer needs and address vendor discontinuation.

Core constraints we had to address

  • Vendor discontinuation of legacy Voyager platform requiring immediate action
  • Complex technical integration: Voyager deeply embedded with dependencies across IT, operations, compliance, and business units
  • High licensing costs for legacy systems creating unnecessary overhead
  • Internal delays in vendor selection slowing the modernization timeline
  • Needed extensive change management across business and technical teams

A practical modernization path

We joined as a strategic implementation partner, providing skilled consultants to support decommissioning and helping M&T reimagine their digital banking experience. We evaluated multiple digital banking vendors using a matrix of 130+ business use cases, facilitated the selection of Q2, and delivered change management including training and documentation.

We carved out the right boundary first so the platform could evolve without unnecessary disruption or migration drag.

How do you modernize a live platform without creating more delivery drag?+

The strongest path is phased modernization. Work starts by isolating the constraint that matters most, then sequencing migration, integration, and change-management decisions so continuity is protected while the new foundation is introduced.

What architecture choices usually matter most in platform modernization work?+

Clear system boundaries, reliable integration behavior, and maintainable technology choices matter more than novelty. In this case, the implementation centered on Q2 Platform, Spring Boot, and AWS.

How do you modernize without turning it into a disruptive big-bang project?+

By treating modernization as a sequenced operating change, not a single technical event. Work starts with the clearest boundary and expands once continuity is protected.

What helps a platform transition feel manageable for the team?+

Clear migration sequencing, explicit ownership, and practical change-management support. This engagement reflected that with emphasis on "Vendor Evaluation & Selection".

What matters most when complexity increases during growth?+

Clear ownership, reliable operating loops, and stronger coordination patterns become critical so growth does not erode delivery confidence.

Conducted comprehensive side-by-side assessments of multiple platforms across scalability, security, UX, and integration capabilities using 130+ business use cases. That mattered because it directly addressed constraints such as: Complex technical integration: Voyager deeply embedded with dependencies across IT, operations, compliance, and business units.
