When teams usually come to us

Typical moments when teams want a clearer next step.

  • 01

    We keep changing direction

    Every week brings a new priority, so we keep restarting instead of finishing.

  • 02

    We are busy, but deadlines keep slipping

    People are working hard, but context switching keeps pushing key work out.

  • 03

    Our systems feel shaky

    One small change creates rework somewhere else, so progress feels fragile.

  • 04

    We are not sure what the next step is

    Before we commit more time, budget, or people, we need a clear call.

  • 05

    Coordination is taking over the week

    Too many handoffs and check-ins are slowing decisions and stalling momentum.

  • 06

    We cannot tell what is actually moving

    The roadmap looks full, but we still struggle to see what actually shipped and helped.

How we can help right now

Choose the kind of help that fits where you are right now.

01
Recommended first step

Founder Review

Assessment before the next major commit

Interviews, review, and a written read with options for leadership.

  • Written assessment and prioritized options.
  • Materials you can take to the board or budget process.

Best when: You need an outside view before you fund the next build or vendor round.

Typical scope: 1–2 weeks.

Explore Founder Review
02

Project Execution

Scoped project with milestones and delivery team

Discovery, plan, and build against a statement of work your team sponsors.

  • Milestone plan and regular demos.
  • Shipped work and handoff docs at the end of each phase.

Best when: The path is clear enough to describe in a short scope document.

Typical scope: 4–10 weeks per phase.

Explore Project Execution
03

Growth Partnership

Ongoing relationship across multiple projects

Retained capacity and one team that stays with you across programs and releases.

  • Named leadership and shared context across projects.
  • Portfolio reviews and faster kickoffs on new work.

Best when: You run several initiatives a year and want continuity, not a fresh vendor each time.

Typical scope: 3+ months (ongoing retainer).
